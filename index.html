Experiment Steps

1

Materials Needed

You only need very basic stuff:

  • Empty plastic bottle (with cap)
  • Balloon
  • Basin or large bowl
  • Hot water
  • Cold water (with ice, if possible)
  • Thermometer (optional but better)
  • Worksheet / observation table
Setup Check

In this simulation, the balloon is already attached to the bottle. In a real experiment, make sure the balloon is tightly sealed over the bottle opening with no air escapes.

2

Cold Water Test

Drag the bottle into the cold water basin or click the "Start Experiment" button to begin automatically.

Observe what happens to the balloon over 2–3 minutes.

Observation Questions

Is the balloon inflated or deflated in cold water?

What does that tell us about the pressure inside the bottle?

3

Hot Water Test

Now move the bottle into the hot water basin or continue with the automatic experiment.

Wait 2–3 minutes and observe the changes to the balloon.

Analysis Questions

What happened to the balloon in hot water?

Did the volume of the bottle change during this experiment?

4

Scientific Discovery

Based on your observations, complete the following statements:

Gay-Lussac's Law Discovery

When temperature increases → pressure ______

When temperature decreases → pressure ______

Volume of the bottle stayed ______

Therefore: Temperature ↑ = Pressure ↑ (at constant volume)

This relationship is known as Gay-Lussac's Law: At constant volume, the pressure of a gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature.

Conclusion

In your own words, explain why the balloon changes size with temperature.
